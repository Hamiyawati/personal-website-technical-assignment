AI in Medical Biotechnology

23 November 2023

AI is extensively utilized in biotechnology to address a range of challenges, encompassing drug discovery, drug safety, functional and structural genomics, proteomics, metabolomics, pharmacology, pharmacogenetics, and pharmacogenomics, among numerous others. The future progress in this field is contingent upon the effective utilization of advanced AI solutions by biotechnology researchers. Currently, the biotechnology industry heavily depends on data storage, filtering, analysis, and sharing. Biotechnology companies and healthcare organizations globally maintain extensive databases. Processes like drug manufacturing, chemical analysis, and the sequencing of RNA and DNA necessitate robust support from AI software solutions for increased efficiency and reduced manual errors.

There are several trending topics in the AI and biotechnology field are actively under research and are expected to remain focal points in the future:

AI/ML and Data Analytics

The increasing significance of AI/ML and data analytics in biotechnology involves the analysis of extensive datasets. These tools are employed to predict complex biological systems, utilizing AI techniques for the examination of genomic data, proteomic data, and various other biological data types. This aids in a deeper understanding of disease mechanisms and the identification of potential therapeutic targets.

Drug Discovery and Development

AI's capacity to analyze vast datasets reveals patterns and relationships not easily discernible by humans. This capability is harnessed for identifying new drugs, drug targets, and optimizing existing therapies.

Personalized Medicine

AI plays a crucial role in analyzing an individual's genomic data and other health information to create personalized treatment plans tailored to specific needs. Machine learning algorithms predict individual responses to treatments and identify potential adverse reactions.

Diagnostics and Disease Prediction

AI analyzes data from diverse sources, including electronic health records and wearable devices, to detect patterns indicating the presence of specific diseases. This improves diagnostic accuracy and enables early interventions to prevent disease progression.

Biomedical Image Analysis

AI is utilized for the analysis of medical images, such as CT scans and MRI images, to identify abnormalities and diagnose diseases. Deep learning algorithms automate the segmentation and classification of structures in medical images.

Source:

Holzinger et al. (2023). AI for life: Trends in artificial intelligence for biotechnology, New Biotechnology. New Biotechnology. 74:16-24.
